Browsing Facebook can really sooth the boredom that you are feeling. When you are not doing anything, Facebook could be a better place to relax. Facebook is not just a simple social media site. 2.3. Twitter

Twitter is one of the popular social media sites that are being used by millions of people around the world. It is an online news and social networking services described by the posts and interactions of messages or tweets limited to 140 characters only. Twitter is run only by exchange of messages and nothing else. This social media site is real time and you can easily have the first news about a particular person at once.

Twitter was created by Jack Dorsey and 3 others last March 2006 where it rapidly became a boom worldwide. Users can access Twitter in any internet based device such as computers and tablets and in any mobile device applications that will enable you to tweet any time. This social media site has changed dramatically over the years and most of the features of this site function almost like Facebook already.

Most of the messages in the Twitter can be viewed publicly but the senders can actually deliver their message only to their followers. It is restricted somehow that only a few people are allowed to have the view of the message. Only those registered users can post tweets and those who are unregistered have the chance to read them. And nothing more.   

Users can subscribe to other tweets and this is the process of following a particular person to keep up with the posts of the user. Any tweets can be forwarded by any user to their newsfeed for the so called retweet. There is also like of any individual tweets. Since it is a social media site, Twitter concepts always involve the so called followers.

When you follow specific user tweets, it will appear in a reverse order on your main Twitter page. This is how Twitter works in the social media accounts. Aside from this, the Twitter has now a Twitter Card Integration that provides convenience in sharing all types of multimedia content on Twitter.

Snapchat was first known as “Picaboo” in its early days as it launched in July 2011 in the iOS store. Headed primarily by Evan Spiegel and Reggie Brown. The concept was first developed by Spiegel when he wanted to make a selfie app that shares imaged of taken selfie pics and deleted themselves afterward. It was only later when Spiegel contacted a coder in Bobby Murphy to write-in the source code that the app would, later on, be known as Snapchat.

Snapchat is so easy to use since it is famous among kids and teenagers. When you have downloaded the app, the first thing you do is to sign up, create and add contacts, open messages apps, all the others you can skip and do it later if you want to go snapping already. When taking “SNAP” you have the option of using the rear or the front cam via the button on the top right corner.

Take a picture by pressing the round button like thing on the lower center part of your screen. Once you have taken the shot, you have the option of tagging your friends and how long they get to see the image because the image does not last that long, too bad. You then get to customize the image by adding filters and captions just by pressing the screen and viola you have your SNAP!

You can now send it to the friends of your choosing. You can also take videos instead of pictures and send or share them with your friends and engage in chats with a snap! Snapchat gives you the chance to share your stories online and be able to share them with the people you want to share them with creating a fun and unique way of taking photos and videos.

2.8. Tumblr

The TUMBLR app is a social networking site that allows posting of shared multimedia and other media content on a short-form blog. Way back in February 2007, it was founded by its CEO David Karp and owned by Oath Inc. The main headquarter of the software app is located in New York City. The company’s parent organizations are Yahoo! and Altaba.

The application was developed after the CEO David Karp was looking for a website that offered a way to write a tumblelog (short-form blogs). After a year without any sign of a new blogging platform, Karp along with Marco Arment invented the application for this purpose. after 2 weeks, the app had an estimated user base of 75 thousand.

How it works

The Dashboard:  This area is the most focused on the part of the tumbler in that this is where most live feeds and recent blogs post are found especially when you follow it. The users are able to re-blog, comment, link, follow and like other posts from different blogs that present itself on the board.

With a single click of the button at the top of the dashboard, the user can post, videos, photos, images, quotes and even text posts to their blog. With the ongoing trend of social media, you can you’re your shared content or status post with Twitter and Facebook.

The Queue: the users have the ability to post anytime they want even delaying and prolonging it.

The Tags:  the tumbler app allows users to tag post for their shared content enabling others to get a look at it afterward.
